     The Yung Ulcer EP is not a project for everyone. It's got a lot of foul lyrics and concepts that will set people off. To some it will be the worst music they've ever heard, to some it will be "FIRE ASF BRO", and to some it will be life changing and eye-opening. But to me, The Yung Ulcer EP is redemption and freedom from the music that I've always wanted to make. I made my first rap songs in 2018, a lot of them silly and all over the place. On this project my ability as a musician has improved ten fold, but the concepts and ideas stay the same. This is the most immature music I've ever heard and there's not a chance the songs featured will ever be heard on the radio, at the club, or performed at a high school talent show.

Now, I want you to open your ears and your mind. Enter the world of Yung Ulcer and the wild life I encompass. Listen to the words I say, the ideas I bring to the table, and the twisted morals present on this project. One of the tracks is an Eminem mockup about a dead superintendent. The outro song is a fatphobia anthem. I don't wish any bad on the family of a late superintendent, may his soul rest in peace, and I don't hate fat people as much as Track 7 might lead you to believe. Some of the kindest and realest people I've ever met are overweight. Yes there is a song called Toddlerf*cker. Contrary to the title, I have no intent on harming a minor. This is all part of the bigger picture, the malice piece, The Yung Ulcer EP.

     Go into this project with a sense of humor, please. You are going to hear some very odd lyrics throughout the seven tracks. You are going to hear sexism, fatphobia, homophobia, the whole nine yards. I have been working on the concepts featured here since 2019, and learned a lot about myself, my music, and the world in the process. If you have any concerns, shoot me an email at jakobjamesutter@gmail.com. Thank you for your time, your patience, and your attention. Enjoy.
